# PRODUCT.md — Selling A Business (Ruchika Tekkeveetil)

## Register
**brand** — marketing site. Design IS the product. The site has to make a BC business owner feel they have found a credible, considered practice for what is likely the most consequential transaction of their working life.

## Product Purpose
A practice-branded website at **sellingabusiness.ca** for a solo commercial REALTOR® practice run by Ruchika Tekkeveetil. The brand is the *practice* ("Selling A Business"), not the person — Ruchika is the founder and only practitioner, but the brand survives a brokerage change. The site exists to:

1. Convert sellers and buyers of BC small / mid-market businesses into a free first consultation via the intake form
2. Establish credibility against larger brokerages (Sunbelt, Pinson) without competing on volume
3. Handle four service lines: business sales, industrial real estate, development land, office & retail leasing/sales
4. Serve as a destination Stanley Shawn (Managing Broker, Realty Alliance Inc.) can approve under BCFSA advertising rules

## Users
**Primary — Sellers.** BC owner-operators, typically 50–70 years old, who built a business over 10–30 years and are starting to think about exit. Revenue range roughly $1M–$25M. Industries: food & hospitality (restaurants, QSR, franchises), skilled trades (HVAC, electrical, construction services), small/mid manufacturing, service businesses with recurring revenue. They sell a business once in their life and the stakes are existential. They've usually been referred by an accountant, a lawyer, or another business owner. They are *not* design-literate; they are skepticism-literate.

**Secondary — Buyers.** First-time acquirers (immigrants, mid-career switchers, family-office adjacents) and experienced operators looking for their next platform.

**Tertiary — Referrers.** CPAs, business lawyers, lenders, and past clients who send referrals.

## Brand & Tone
- **Practice-led brand, not personal name.** Wordmark is "Selling A Business" set in confident sans-serif. Ruchika appears as Founder & Commercial REALTOR® in the About section and contact pages. The brand stays even if she changes brokerages.
- **Considered, rooted, adult.** Plain English headlines. No metaphors. No poetic language. No "Where Business Meets Its Next Chapter."
- **Photo-driven, not type-driven.** Every section has a photograph. Business contexts: warehouses, paperwork, factories, equipment, logistics, lease agreements, construction sites, hospitality interiors, Vancouver/Lower Mainland scenes. Photos are subtly graded so they unify aesthetically.
- **Confidentiality is foreground.** "Confidential" is prominent everywhere a contact form appears.

## Aesthetic direction
- **Primary reference:** Benjamin Hardman (benjaminhardman.com) — geometric modern sans-serif, restraint, photo-driven layout, picture / text / picture / text rhythm
- **Secondary references:** Will Architects-style boutique-practice sites (per Ruchika's call) — white background, photo-led, simple structure
- **Typography:** Single typeface — PP Neue Montreal (preferred) or Switzer (alternative), both free, both geometric modern sans. Used at different weights for hierarchy. No decorative or secondary fonts.
- **Color:** Warm ivory background (`oklch(97.5% 0.005 75)`, ~`#FAF8F4`), near-black text (`oklch(15% 0.01 75)`, ~`#1C1814`), soft warm-gray borders. Single warm clay accent (`oklch(45% 0.13 35)`, ~`#993822`) used sparingly. **No blue, no navy** — explicitly rejected by Ruchika.

## Anti-references
- **Luxury wedding/jewelry editorial** — Cormorant, gold accents, cream-and-gold, italic flourishes. Earlier version of this site fell into this trap and was rejected.
- **AI-glossy SaaS landing** — gradient backgrounds, glassmorphism, hero-metric template (big number / small label / supporting stats), identical-card grids, count-up animations. The previous slate/Inter version was described by Ruchika as "looks like a presentation."
- **Volume brokerage corporate** — sterile blue gradients, "Our Team" of 40 photos, generic stock handshakes presented uncritically.
- **Realtor-personal-brand** — house photo backgrounds, "Your trusted realtor" tagline, bright real-estate-yellow accents, big agent face overlaid on a sold sign.
- **Generic Squarespace template** — even though Squarespace templates inspired the references, the site is custom-built for distinctiveness and durability.

## Strategic principles
- **Multiple CTAs everywhere.** Per Ruchika, every page section ends with "Start a confidential conversation." Capture leads from anywhere.
- **Brochure is the content source.** Copy is adapted from `Strategic-Advisory-for-Buying-and-Selling-Businesses-in-BC.pdf` — but only her sections. Michael Hwang, RE/MAX Commercial Advantage, and Professional Business Exchange Inc. (ProBX) are removed entirely. "We / our partnership" rewritten as "I / my practice."
- **Lower Mainland only.** No Okanagan, no Vancouver Island. Service area is Lower Mainland of British Columbia, period.
- **Real testimonials.** Three real Google Reviews already exist on her GMB (Naina Gandevia, Rajeena Khara, Holly Li) — these are public, attributable, and BCFSA-compliant.
- **Confidentiality as voice.** The intake form leads with "Confidential consultation." Form is simplified to remove pushy/intrusive questions (profitability, reason for selling, timeline). Response window: 24 to 48 hours.

## Brokerage compliance — per Ruchika's direction
